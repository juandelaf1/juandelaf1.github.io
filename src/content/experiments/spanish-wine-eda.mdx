## Overview

An exploratory analysis of the Spanish wine market using a dataset of 10,000+ wines across 60+ regions. The analysis examined relationships between price, rating, region, and grape variety to identify quality-value sweet spots.

## Key Findings

- **Regional price variation**: Wines from Ribera del Duero commanded a 40% premium over comparable wines from other regions
- **Price-rating correlation**: Weak positive correlation (r = 0.34)—price is a poor predictor of quality
- **Value regions**: Several DOs (Denominaciones de Origen) produced consistently high-rated wines at below-average prices

This experiment informed feature engineering decisions in later production projects where categorical variables with many levels (60+ regions) required careful encoding strategies.
