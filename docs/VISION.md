# Vision

## Professional Identity

I build data products. End to end. From raw data to production APIs and interactive systems.

This is not a job title. It is a category of engineer that the industry is still learning to name. Data Product Engineer. Someone who understands business operations AND production engineering. Someone who designs systems, not just queries.

## Long-Term Vision

This website must grow with my career for the next 5-10 years.

- Today: Data Analyst positioning with full-stack data engineering skills
- Tomorrow: Senior Data Product Engineer / Lead Data Platform Engineer
- Future: references to team leadership, system design at scale, platform architecture

The architecture must support this evolution without structural rewrites.

## Core Mission

When a recruiter, engineering manager, or CTO visits this website, they must think within 60 seconds:

> "This person knows how to build production-ready data products that solve real business problems. They understand business constraints AND engineering rigor. They think in systems, not scripts."

## Target Audience

Primary:
- Technical recruiters at product/tech companies
- Engineering managers (data platforms, analytics engineering)
- CTOs / VPs of Engineering at data-intensive startups
- Senior data engineers reviewing candidates

Secondary:
- Technical co-founders evaluating potential partners
- Clients evaluating freelance/contract data engineering

## What This Website Communicates

- Engineering maturity: production thinking, testing, CI/CD, Docker, documentation
- Business understanding: domain expertise in operations, costs, margins, forecasting
- Product thinking: end-to-end ownership, measurable outcomes, user-centric design
- Technical depth: Python, SQL, FastAPI, PostgreSQL, ML pipelines, cloud
- Communication clarity: writing like engineering docs, not like a blog

## What It Must Never Become

- A collection of dashboards without context
- A list of Jupyter notebooks selling "analysis"
- A tutorial site teaching beginners
- A resume in HTML format
- A flashy landing page with animations and particles
- A template that looks like every other portfolio
- A place where I talk about "passion" instead of results
- A site with fake metrics, placeholder content, or "under construction" pages
